WHIM COMMUNITY GUIDELINES

Last Updated: April 24, 2026
Effective Date: April 24, 2026

Welcome to Whim. Our platform exists to cure boredom by connecting university students with spontaneous real-world events happening right now. To keep Whim a safe, authentic, and enjoyable space for everyone, we ask every member of our community to follow these guidelines. These rules apply to all content and conduct on Whim, including event listings, event chat messages, profile information, images, and in-person behavior connected to Whim events.

These Community Guidelines are part of your agreement with Whim and are incorporated into our Terms of Service. Violations may result in content removal, account suspension, or permanent ban.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. OUR CORE VALUES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Whim is built on four core values:

AUTHENTICITY — Be real. Use your real identity, post real events, and represent yourself honestly. Whim is not a place for personas, fake events, or deceptive behavior.

SAFETY — The safety of our community comes first. Every feature of Whim is designed with user safety in mind, and we expect users to prioritize safety — their own and others' — in every interaction.

SPONTANEITY — Whim is about seizing the moment. Events are short-lived, last-minute, and local. Respect the spirit of the platform by keeping events genuine and timely.

INCLUSIVITY — University life brings together people of diverse backgrounds, identities, and experiences. Whim is a welcoming space for all students, regardless of race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, gender identity, sexual orientation, age, disability status, or any other characteristic.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. WHO CAN USE WHIM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

2.1 Eligibility. Whim may require institutional email verification (such as a .edu address) for registration or certain features. Where such verification is required, you must provide a valid institutional email address you are authorized to use and accurately represent any associated affiliation. Eligibility requirements may change at Whim's discretion.

2.2 Age Requirement. You must be at least 18 years of age to use Whim. Persons under 18 are not permitted to use the Service.

2.3 Identity Honesty. You are expected to use your real name and represent your identity honestly on Whim. Pseudonyms, aliases, and fictional personas are not permitted. While Whim does not independently verify user identity beyond email verification, misrepresenting your identity is a violation of these guidelines.

2.4 One Account. Each person may have only one Whim account. Creating additional accounts to evade enforcement, access a banned account, or misrepresent your identity is strictly prohibited.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. EVENT STANDARDS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Events are the heart of Whim. All events must meet the following standards:

3.1 Events Must Be Real. Every event you post must be a genuine activity that is actually going to happen. Do not post fake events, placeholder events, or events you have no intention of hosting.

3.2 Events Must Happen Within 24 Hours. Events on Whim are intended to be spontaneous and near-future. All events must be scheduled to occur within 24 hours of posting.

3.3 Accurate Information Required. Your event's title, description, time, and location must be accurate and truthful. The location you provide must be where the event will actually take place, as our platform uses GPS verification for attendance.

3.4 No Misleading or Bait Events. Do not create events with misleading titles, descriptions, or images designed to attract attendees under false pretenses. Do not use an event to collect attendees and then redirect them to something they did not agree to.

3.5 No Charging Attendees Without Disclosure. If your event has an entry fee, cover charge, or any cost to attendees, you must clearly disclose this in the event description before people join. Surprise fees are prohibited.

3.6 No Events for Illegal Activity. Events must not be created to facilitate illegal activities of any kind. This includes but is not limited to:
   - Unlawful distribution, sale, or use of controlled substances
   - Underage drinking events (events where the primary purpose involves alcohol for under-21 participants)
   - Illegal gambling
   - Trespassing or events at locations you are not authorized to use
   - Events designed to facilitate harm to others

3.7 Respect Venue Restrictions. If you are hosting an event at a university building, campus space, or private venue, you are responsible for ensuring you have appropriate authorization from the venue.

3.8 Event Images. Cover images for events must not contain nudity, graphic violence, hateful imagery, or other prohibited content as defined in our Content Policy.

3.9 One Event at a Time. Do not flood the platform with duplicate or near-duplicate events. Creating multiple listings for the same event is prohibited.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. SOCIAL INTERACTION STANDARDS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.1 Treat Everyone With Respect. Every Whim user is a real person. Treat all users with basic dignity and respect, whether in group chats, through direct interaction, or at in-person events.

4.2 No Harassment. Harassment of any kind is strictly prohibited. This includes:
   - Persistent unwanted contact or messages
   - Threats of any kind, whether physical, legal, or reputational
   - Intimidation or attempts to make someone fear for their safety
   - Targeted ridicule, mockery, or humiliation
   - Sending repeated messages after someone has indicated they do not wish to communicate

4.3 No Discrimination or Hate Speech. Whim is an inclusive platform. Content that demeans, dehumanizes, or attacks individuals or groups based on their race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, age, disability, or any other protected characteristic is strictly prohibited.

4.4 No Sexual Harassment or Unwanted Advances. Unsolicited romantic or sexual advances, comments of a sexual nature directed at another user without their consent, or sexually explicit messages are prohibited.

4.5 No Bullying. Whim is not a place to gang up on, single out, or bully other users. Coordinated attacks or campaigns against individual users are prohibited.

4.6 No Stalking. Do not use information obtained through Whim to track, monitor, or follow another user's movements or activities against their wishes.

4.7 Respect Boundaries. If another user blocks you, do not attempt to contact them through other means, create new accounts to circumvent the block, or show up to their events.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. CHAT STANDARDS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.1 Event Chats Are Shared Spaces. Event group chats are visible to all attendees of an event. Remember that your messages are seen by everyone in the group.

5.2 Messages Are Permanent. Once sent, chat messages cannot be edited or deleted by users. Our platform intentionally preserves chat history for the duration of an event. Think carefully before you send a message. Be thoughtful and consider how your words will be received.

5.3 Stay On Topic. Chats are connected to specific events. Keep your messages relevant to the event. Excessive off-topic content or flooding a chat with repetitive messages is prohibited.

5.4 No Spam or Self-Promotion. Do not use event chats to advertise businesses, solicit followers or subscribers, distribute affiliate links, or promote services unrelated to the event.

5.5 No Sharing Others' Private Information. Do not share another person's private information — including their contact details, address, or anything they shared in confidence — in a group chat or elsewhere on the platform.

5.6 No Hate Speech or Slurs. The prohibition on hateful content applies equally to chat messages. Slurs, derogatory language, and hate speech are not tolerated in event chats.

5.7 System Messages. Certain automated messages (e.g., "[User] joined the event") are generated by the platform and displayed in event chats. These are informational and cannot be disabled.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. PROFILE STANDARDS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

6.1 Honest Identity. You are expected to represent your genuine identity on your profile, including using your real first and last name. Whim does not independently verify user identities beyond email verification, but misrepresentation is a violation of these guidelines. Do not impersonate another real person, fictional character, celebrity, Whim employee, or any organization.

6.2 Accurate University Affiliation. Your listed university must be the institution associated with your registered email address. Do not claim affiliation with an institution you are not actually affiliated with.

6.3 Appropriate Profile and Banner Images. Profile photos and banner images must be appropriate for a general university audience. The following are prohibited as profile or banner images:
   - Nudity or sexually explicit imagery
   - Graphic violence or gore
   - Hateful symbols, logos, or imagery
   - Images designed to impersonate another person
   - Images of minors in inappropriate contexts

6.4 Clubs and Organizations. If you list club or organization affiliations, they must be real organizations at your institution that you genuinely participate in. Do not fabricate affiliations.

6.5 Accurate Major and Year. Academic information you provide (major, graduation year) should be reasonably accurate. We understand information may change, but deliberately providing false academic information to manipulate how you are perceived on the platform is not permitted.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. REPORTING VIOLATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

If you encounter a user or event that violates these Community Guidelines, please use Whim's in-app reporting tools:

To report a user: Visit their profile and select the report option. You may report for the following reasons:
   - Spam
   - Harassment
   - Inappropriate Content
   - Fake Profile
   - Other (provide a description)

To report an event: If you believe an event violates our guidelines, report it through the event's reporting option.

What happens after you report:
1. Your report is submitted to our moderation team
2. Reports are reviewed on a priority basis, with safety-related reports receiving the highest priority
3. We will investigate and take appropriate action
4. We will not disclose the outcome of investigations to reporters to protect user privacy

Do not file false or bad-faith reports. Abusing the reporting system to harass other users is itself a violation of these guidelines.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. ENFORCEMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

8.1 Enforcement Tiers. When we determine that a violation has occurred, we may take the following actions depending on the severity and frequency of the violation:

Content Removal: Removing violating content (event listings, images, or other posts) without suspension.

Warning: Issuing a formal warning to your account, which may be considered in future enforcement decisions.

Temporary Suspension: Suspending your ability to access some or all features of the Service for a defined period (e.g., 7 days, 30 days).

Permanent Ban: Permanently removing your account and all associated data from the Service. Permanent bans are issued for severe violations (e.g., posting illegal content, repeated serious harassment) or repeated violations after warnings.

8.2 Immediate Action. Certain severe violations — including posting child sexual abuse material (CSAM), making credible threats of violence, or coordinated harassment campaigns — may result in immediate permanent account termination without prior warning.

8.3 Context and Severity. We consider context when making enforcement decisions. We distinguish between minor, isolated violations and patterns of harmful conduct. Repeated violations escalate the response.

8.4 Appeals. If you believe an enforcement action against your account was taken in error, you may appeal by emailing appeals@joinwhim.net within 30 days of the action. Include your registered email address and a description of why you believe the action was incorrect. Whim will review your appeal and respond within 15 business days. We are not obligated to reverse our decision.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. ALCOHOL, SUBSTANCES, AND SENSITIVE EVENTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

9.1 Alcohol. Events involving alcohol are permitted only when all participants are of legal drinking age (21+ in the United States) and the event complies with all applicable laws. Events must not be designed to pressure, coerce, or incentivize alcohol consumption. Do not use Whim to advertise drinking games, drinking challenges, or any event whose primary purpose is intoxication.

9.2 Controlled Substances. Events involving the use or distribution of controlled substances (illegal drugs) are strictly prohibited, regardless of whether such substances are decriminalized or legal in any particular jurisdiction.

9.3 Sensitive Events. Events related to mental health, grief, crisis support, or other sensitive topics are permitted and welcome, but must be clearly labeled and must adhere to our Content Policy. Do not exploit sensitive topics to attract participants under false pretenses.

9.4 Events Involving Physical Activity or Risk. If your event involves physical activity (sports, hiking, etc.) with inherent risk, you are strongly encouraged to disclose this in your event description. You are responsible for ensuring participants are aware of any risks.
